Glass in all its forms…

In the framework of the International Year of Glass 2022, the Lycée Jean Monnet – École Nationale du Verre and the Union pour la Science et la Technologie Verrières (USTV) organized a week dedicated to glass science, education, craftsmanship and innovation.

This event brought together teachers, students, artists, researchers and R&D engineers around conferences, workshops and discussions about glass and its professions.

Students

A total of 145 students and young people in glass-related training programmes took part in the meeting. They came from different educational pathways dedicated to glass, including CAP Arts et Techniques du Verre (option Décorateur sur verre), CAP Arts du Verre et du Cristal, BMA Décorateur sur verre, BMA Souffleur de verre and DNMADe Matériaux – Créateur Verrier. The event also involved students from other programmes at Lycée Jean Monnet, particularly those in Bac Professionnel Métiers de l’accueil and hospitality and catering courses (CAP and Bac Professionnel). They contributed to the organisation of the event by welcoming participants, managing registration, preparing badges, guiding visitors and helping coordinate workshops and meals. This collaboration allowed students from different fields to participate in a unique exchange between glass science, craftsmanship, artistic creation and event organisation.

The students from the Bac Professionnel Métiers de l’accueil were responsible for welcoming participants, badges, signage, workshop management and organisation of meals.

The invited speakers were welcomed for lunches and dinners in the educational restaurants of Lycée Jean Monnet.

The Conferences

Each day, participants attended two conferences. The programme explored glass from its history and composition to modern applications and recycling.

  • Day 1: History of glass and main components
  • Day 2: Structure of glass and colours
  • Day 3: Mechanical properties of glass
  • Day 4: Design, art and craftsmanship
  • Day 5: Current uses, life cycle and recycling

Four glass artists also presented their artistic approach and their work related to the conference themes.

The Workshops

The workshops were designed as practical and interactive sessions bringing together students, teachers, researchers and glass professionals. They provided an opportunity for participants to exchange knowledge, share experiences and discover glass from both technical and scientific perspectives.

Students from glass training programmes were at the heart of these exchanges. They worked alongside artists, craftsmen and scientists, combining professional glassmaking techniques with a deeper understanding of the physical and chemical properties of glass.

The workshops were organised around two main themes:

1. Hot and cold glass techniques

Participants discovered professional glassmaking processes through hands-on activities, combining technical practice with exchanges between students, glass professionals and researchers.

Hot glass workshop

Glass gathering, stretching, Prince Rupert’s drops and float/bomma experiments.
Led by F. Capet, A. Mexmain and D. Marcade

Glass working with a torch

Introduction to shaping and transforming glass using a burner.
Led by B. Darrieutort

Fusing

Glass assembly, annealing, devitrification and tempering processes.
Led by J. Bloux and Y. Gueguen

Stained glass

Cutting, assembling, grisaille painting and glass decoration techniques.
Led by S. Denizard, A. Regue and J. Kellog

Glass decoration

Shaping, cutting, sandblasting, bouchardage, grinding and polishing techniques.
Led by G. Gatell, L. Regnault and C. Nika

2. Physical chemistry of glass

Through experiments and demonstrations, students explored the scientific principles that govern glass properties, including colour, structure, optical behaviour and melting.

Colorimetry

Nadia Pellerin

Optical spectrum of glasses

Gérald Lelong

Colouring glass: fusion experiments

Laurent Cormier

Studying glass structure using Raman spectroscopy

Daniel Neuville

Observing glass melting processes

Making glass using a microwave oven

These workshops created a unique dialogue between students, researchers, teachers and glass professionals. By combining practical experience, scientific investigation and artistic approaches, participants discovered glass as both a material of innovation and a medium for creativity.

Exhibition of Glass Artists' Works

Four glass artists: Roselyne Blanc-Bessière, Yves Braun, Antoine Rault and Jonathan Ausseresse presented their works at the EROA exhibition space of Lycée Jean Monnet.

The exhibition was displayed alongside creations made by students of the school from 01 to 08 April 2022.

The public exhibition was inaugurated on Tuesday 05 April by the Rector of the Clermont-Ferrand Academy and Jérôme De Lavergnolle, President of the Fédération du Verre et du Cristal.
