Glass Days 2023: Exploring viscosity, glass processing and innovative raw materials

The 2023 Glass Days were dedicated to exploring the behaviour of glass at high temperature, with a particular focus on viscosity, a fundamental parameter controlling glass processing and shaping. The event brought together students, teachers, researchers and glass professionals to discuss the relationships between viscosity, temperature and glassworking practices, building on experimental measurements performed on laboratory on the glass composition used at Lycée Jean Monnet – École Nationale du Verre.

Experimental temperature measurements were carried out to determine the working temperature ranges of the glass and to compare them with viscosity–temperature curves obtained through laboratory investigations. These studies aimed to identify the optimal viscosity–temperature domains required for different glassworking operations, from forming and shaping to finishing processes.

Beyond the study of processing conditions, the 2023 Glass Days also opened new perspectives on the development of sustainable glass compositions through the exploration of local raw materials. First trials were conducted using mixtures of glass and locally sourced volcanic lava, investigating the potential of natural resources to create innovative compositions while assessing their influence on glass properties and processing behaviour.

Translating exchanges into research opportunities

The exchanges and collaborative activities carried out during these days proved highly fruitful, strengthening the connections between research, education and glassmaking practice. The discussions and experimental work provided valuable insights into the links between glass composition, viscosity and processing conditions, and contributed to the emergence of new research directions.

This collaborative dynamic ultimately led to the submission of an ANR proposal within the call “Science with and for Society – Innovative Ambitions”, with the ambition of further developing interactions between scientists, teachers and students around the future of glass materials.
